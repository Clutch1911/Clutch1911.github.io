Michael Robert Merola

I'm a Colorado native hailing from snowy Boulder, CO. I am a student, a musician, an actor, a fencer, an artist, a developer, and a thinker. This webpage presents the details of myself, my work, and my interests. Contact me if you want to talk! Thanks.

Currently, I am a Computer Science major at the University of Colorado Boulder. I know how to program in C++, Python, Java, HTML/CSS. I have some drafting experience in AutoCAD from my summer internship with Schendt Engineering. I use Unity 5 and GameMaker Studio to develop games, and I am learning to develop moblie apps with React Native.

I have attended two hackathons. The first was the Broncos TackleSTEM hackathon in Denver, where I worked with colleagues to create Amazon Alexa skills using the CleverBot API and AWS Lambda. Our project won the 'Best Demo' Award. I also learned basic machine learning and markov chains with markovify to create simple Obama/Trump bots that will produce randomly generated phrases based on data gathered from public speeches. The second was a local hack day for HackCU, where I spent most of my time learning how to use React Native for app development. Check out my Devpost and GitHub for more info on my projects.

My biggest passion is music. I have been an avid guitarist since age 10, playing everything from Led Zeppelin to Wes Montgomery. I play the clarinet as 6th chair in the CU Boulder Concert Band. I also play some tenor sax, bass guitar, and drums. Currently, I am training to be a better jazz guitarist and working on my first self-produced album. Check out my SoundCloud to keep up with new tracks!

Acting is another passion of mine. I love to act in plays and sometimes musicals. In high school, I performed as a supporting role in Neil Simon's The Odd Couple and as the lead role of Pip in Charles Dickens' Great Expectations.
